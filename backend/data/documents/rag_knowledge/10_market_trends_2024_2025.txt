DUBAI REAL ESTATE MARKET TRENDS — 2024 TO 2025
================================================

MARKET OVERVIEW
Dubai's real estate market has experienced extraordinary growth since 2020, driven by:
- Global investor confidence in Dubai's stability
- Government initiatives (Golden Visa, 100% foreign business ownership)
- Strong economic fundamentals and tourism recovery
- Net migration of high-net-worth individuals to Dubai
- Limited supply in premium areas vs strong demand

TRANSACTION VOLUMES
2023 records:
- Total transactions: 120,000+ (record year)
- Total value: AED 400+ billion (record)
- Foreign buyer transactions: 65% of total
- Top nationalities: Indian, British, Russian, Chinese, European

2024 H1 performance:
- 15% increase in transaction volumes vs H1 2023
- Off-plan continues to dominate (60% of transactions)
- Villa prices up 18% year-on-year
- Apartment prices up 12% year-on-year

PRICE TRENDS BY AREA
Premium Areas (Palm Jumeirah, Emirates Hills):
- 2022: AED 1,800-3,500/sqft
- 2024: AED 3,000-7,000/sqft (+70-100% appreciation)
- Demand: Ultra-high-net-worth individuals, wealth migration

Mid-Market (Dubai Marina, Downtown):
- 2022: AED 1,200-2,000/sqft
- 2024: AED 1,600-3,000/sqft (+30-50% appreciation)
- Demand: Professionals, mid-term investors

Affordable (JVC, DSO, International City):
- 2022: AED 500-900/sqft
- 2024: AED 700-1,200/sqft (+25-35% appreciation)
- Demand: First-time buyers, value investors

RENTAL MARKET
- Rental prices up 20-35% since 2022
- Vacancy rates: Below 5% in most popular areas
- Short-term rental (Airbnb): 15-20% premium over long-term
- Corporate housing demand: High due to company relocations

KEY DRIVERS FOR CONTINUED GROWTH
1. Population growth: UAE targets 6M Dubai residents by 2040 (currently ~3.5M)
2. Expo City Dubai legacy: 5,000+ businesses established post-Expo
3. Financial hub status: DIFC growth, FinTech hub
4. Aviation hub: DXB busiest airport globally, Al Maktoum expansion
5. Tourism: 20M+ visitors target for 2025
6. Business-friendly: 0% personal income tax, 100% foreign ownership

SUPPLY PIPELINE 2024-2026
Major upcoming project deliveries:
- Sobha Hartland 2: 3,000+ units
- Emaar Creek Harbour: 10,000+ units
- Dubai Hills: 2,000+ villas/townhouses
- Dubai South: 20,000+ units planned
- JVC new towers: 50+ under construction

EXPERT OUTLOOK 2025
- Price appreciation: 8-12% expected for established areas
- Villa market: Expected to remain strong (supply limited)
- Apartment market: Possible slight softening due to new supply
- Off-plan sales: Expected to remain dominant (50%+ of market)
- Rental yields: Expected to remain stable at 5-8% for apartments
- International demand: Expected to grow as Dubai solidifies financial hub status

RISKS TO MONITOR
- Global economic slowdown impact on investor confidence
- Interest rate environment affecting affordability
- New supply pipeline if overbuilt
- Geopolitical factors in the region
- Regulatory changes
