DUBAI PROPERTY MANAGEMENT — COMPLETE GUIDE
===========================================

WHY USE A PROPERTY MANAGEMENT COMPANY?
If you live outside UAE or don't want the hassle of managing tenants:
- They handle everything: finding tenants, rent collection, maintenance, renewals
- Especially important for holiday home (Airbnb-type) management
- Removes day-to-day stress of being a landlord from abroad

SERVICES PROVIDED
Standard Property Management:
- Tenant screening and placement
- Tenancy contract preparation
- Ejari registration
- Rent collection
- Maintenance coordination
- Annual renewal negotiations
- Reporting and statements

Premium/Full Service (adds):
- Regular property inspections
- DEWA management
- Service charge payments
- Snagging and defect management
- Lease renewal negotiations

Holiday Home Management:
- DTCM permit application
- Listing on Airbnb, Booking.com, VRBO
- Guest communication and check-in/out
- Cleaning and linen management
- Dynamic pricing (adjusts nightly rate by demand)
- Revenue reporting

MANAGEMENT FEE STRUCTURE
Long-term rental management:
- Standard: 5% of annual rent
- Premium: 8-10% of annual rent
- Tenant placement only (one-time): 5% of first year's rent

Holiday home management:
- Commission: 15-25% of gross revenue
- Setup fee: AED 2,000-5,000 (photography, listing creation)
- Cleaning: AED 150-350 per turnover (charged to revenue)
- DTCM permit: AED 1,370/year (you pay)

CHOOSING A PROPERTY MANAGER — CHECKLIST
□ RERA licensed (for long-term rental management)
□ DTCM licensed (for holiday home management)
□ References from other landlords available?
□ Online portal to view statements and bookings?
□ How many properties do they manage? (too many = less attention)
□ Average occupancy rate for similar properties?
□ How are maintenance issues handled and approved?
□ Monthly or quarterly payment to landlord?
□ What happens if tenant stops paying?

RED FLAGS:
- No RERA licence
- Cannot show sample monthly statements
- No clear contract with fee structure
- Asking you to sign power of attorney you haven't read
- No insurance for managed properties

LANDLORD RIGHTS IN DUBAI
- Right to 12 months notice before eviction (for personal use)
- Right to increase rent per RERA index only
- Right to evict for non-payment (after RDSC process)
- Right to inspect property with 24-48 hours notice
- Right to withhold security deposit for damages beyond fair wear and tear

TENANT RIGHTS IN DUBAI
- Right to receive property in good condition
- Right to 90 days notice before rent increase
- Right to peaceful enjoyment (no unannounced visits)
- Right to renew tenancy (unless landlord has valid RERA reason to evict)
- Right to Ejari registration
