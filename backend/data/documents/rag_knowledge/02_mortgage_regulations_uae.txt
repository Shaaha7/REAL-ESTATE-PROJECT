UAE MORTGAGE REGULATIONS — COMPLETE GUIDE
==========================================

UAE CENTRAL BANK MORTGAGE CAPS (LTV LIMITS)
The UAE Central Bank regulates all mortgage lending through Circular 28/2013.

FOR UAE NATIONALS:
- First property up to AED 5M: maximum 80% LTV (20% down payment)
- First property above AED 5M: maximum 70% LTV (30% down payment)
- Second/additional properties: maximum 65% LTV

FOR UAE RESIDENTS (EXPATS):
- First property up to AED 5M: maximum 75% LTV (25% down payment)
- First property above AED 5M: maximum 65% LTV (35% down payment)
- Second/additional properties: maximum 60% LTV

FOR NON-RESIDENTS:
- Maximum 50% LTV (50% down payment required)
- Very limited lenders offer non-resident mortgages
- Higher interest rates apply

OFF-PLAN PROPERTIES
- Most banks do not mortgage off-plan properties under construction
- Construction finance available through developers at higher rates
- Some banks offer "under construction" mortgages from 50% completion

DEBT BURDEN RATIO (DBR)
UAE Central Bank mandates:
- Maximum DBR: 50% of gross monthly income
- Includes all existing debt obligations (car loans, personal loans, credit cards)
- Calculation: Total monthly debt payments / Gross monthly income x 100

MORTGAGE TENURE LIMITS
- UAE Nationals: Maximum 30 years
- Expats: Maximum 25 years
- Combined age limit: Borrower age + tenure ≤ 65 years (salary) or 70 years (self-employed)

INTEREST RATES (2024-2025 APPROXIMATE RANGES)
- Fixed rate (1-5 years): 3.5% to 5.5% per annum
- Variable rate: EIBOR + 1.5% to 2.5%
- Islamic (Ijara/Murabaha): Comparable to conventional rates
- Major lenders: Emirates NBD, ADCB, FAB, Mashreq, HSBC, Standard Chartered

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED FOR MORTGAGE APPLICATION
For Salaried Employees:
- Valid passport + residence visa
- Emirates ID
- Last 3-6 months salary certificates
- Last 6 months bank statements
- Employment contract
- Proof of address

For Self-Employed/Business Owners:
- Last 2 years audited financial statements
- Trade licence
- Last 12 months bank statements
- Memorandum of Association

MORTGAGE PRE-APPROVAL (IN-PRINCIPLE APPROVAL)
- Valid for 60-90 days
- Required before making offers in competitive market
- Does not guarantee final approval
- Free of charge from most banks

ISLAMIC MORTGAGE PRODUCTS
- Ijara: Bank owns property, customer pays rent that reduces principal
- Murabaha: Bank buys property and sells to customer at higher price
- Musharaka: Diminishing partnership arrangement
- All Sharia-compliant, rates similar to conventional mortgages
