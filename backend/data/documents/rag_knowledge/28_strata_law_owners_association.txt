STRATA LAW AND OWNERS ASSOCIATIONS — DUBAI
===========================================

JOINTLY OWNED PROPERTY LAW
Dubai Law No. 27 of 2007 governs all jointly owned properties.
Jointly owned = any development with shared common areas (apartments, gated communities).

OWNERS ASSOCIATION (OA) — WHAT IS IT?
Every jointly owned development MUST have a registered Owners Association.
OA is a legal entity representing all property owners in that development.
Registered with and supervised by RERA.

OA STRUCTURE
Developer phase (before 75% units sold):
- Developer acts as interim OA manager
- Must register with RERA within 60 days of first sale

After 75% sold:
- Elections held — owners vote for OA board
- Minimum 3, maximum 7 board members
- Board members must be owners in the development
- Board elects: Chairman, Vice-Chairman, Secretary, Treasurer

Annual General Meeting (AGM):
- Required by law every year
- 30 days written notice to all owners
- Agenda: Budget approval, board elections, major decisions
- Quorum: 25% of owners (or 10% for second notice if first meeting fails)

OA MANAGEMENT COMPANY
OA hires a licensed management company (not the same as developer):
- Licensed by RERA
- Manages day-to-day operations
- Prepares annual budget
- Collects service charges
- Coordinates maintenance contractors

BUDGET AND SERVICE CHARGES
Management company prepares annual budget:
- General Fund: Day-to-day expenses (cleaning, security, landscaping, utilities)
- Reserve Fund: Future major repairs (10-15% of total budget minimum)
- Budget must be approved at AGM
- Service charges calculated per sqft based on approved budget

RERA DISPUTE RESOLUTION FOR SERVICE CHARGES
If you disagree with service charge amount:
1. Write to OA/management company first
2. If unresolved: File complaint with RERA (RDC — Registration and Dispute Centre)
3. RERA can review and adjust if charges are unreasonable
4. Maximum charges are guided by RERA published rates

COMMON OA RULES (COMMUNITY REGULATIONS)
Every development has House Rules. Common rules:
- No pets (unless building is pet-friendly)
- No commercial activities from residential units
- Noise restrictions (quiet hours 10pm-8am typically)
- Common area usage rules (pool hours, gym rules)
- Moving/delivery time restrictions (weekdays, working hours only)
- Parking rules (no double parking, no visitor parking abuse)
- Modification approval required (before any structural changes)

MODIFICATION TO YOUR UNIT
Interior modifications:
- Painting walls: Usually allowed without approval
- Removing non-structural walls: Need OA + Municipality approval
- Adding furniture: No approval needed
- AC replacement: Same brand/type usually fine, different type needs approval

Exterior modifications (strictly controlled):
- Balcony enclosure: Usually prohibited without full OA + DDA approval
- Satellite dish installation: Usually prohibited or restricted zones only
- External colour changes: Strictly prohibited

FAILING OA SITUATIONS
Signs of a badly managed OA:
- Building maintenance ignored (lifts breaking frequently)
- Security staff not paid → walkouts
- Swimming pool not maintained
- Fire safety equipment not serviced
- Accounts not audited

What you can do:
1. Attend AGM and vote for change
2. Nominate yourself or trusted owner for board
3. Report to RERA (RERA can intervene, audit, or appoint new management)
4. In extreme cases: Court order to force compliance

SERVICE CHARGE NON-PAYMENT
Owners who don't pay:
- Interest penalty: Stated in OA bylaws (typically 1-2%/month)
- Cannot sell property until charges cleared
- OA can take legal action in Dubai courts
- Name may be reported to credit bureaus
