SERVICE CHARGES AND COMMUNITY LIVING IN DUBAI
===============================================

WHAT ARE SERVICE CHARGES?
Service charges (also called maintenance fees) are annual fees paid by property owners
to maintain and operate common areas in jointly owned developments. These are mandatory
and are regulated by RERA under the Jointly Owned Property Law.

HOW SERVICE CHARGES ARE CALCULATED
- Based on the property's share of the total building/community
- Typically charged per square foot of the property
- Approved annually by RERA
- Divided into: General fund (routine maintenance) + Reserve fund (major replacements)

TYPICAL SERVICE CHARGE RATES (2024):
APARTMENTS:
- Downtown Dubai: AED 18-25 per sqft
- Dubai Marina: AED 12-18 per sqft
- Palm Jumeirah Apartments: AED 20-28 per sqft
- Business Bay: AED 12-17 per sqft
- JLT: AED 12-16 per sqft
- JVC: AED 10-14 per sqft
- Dubai Silicon Oasis: AED 9-12 per sqft
- Discovery Gardens: AED 8-11 per sqft

VILLAS AND TOWNHOUSES:
- Emirates Hills: AED 25-35 per sqft
- Palm Jumeirah Villas: AED 20-30 per sqft
- Arabian Ranches: AED 4-7 per sqft
- The Springs/Meadows: AED 4-6 per sqft
- Dubai Hills Estate: AED 6-10 per sqft
- DAMAC Hills: AED 5-8 per sqft

EXAMPLE CALCULATION:
2BR apartment in Dubai Marina, 1,200 sqft at AED 15/sqft:
Annual service charge = 1,200 x 15 = AED 18,000 per year (AED 1,500/month)

WHAT DO SERVICE CHARGES COVER?
Common Area Maintenance:
- Cleaning of lobbies, corridors, parking areas
- Landscaping and garden maintenance
- Swimming pool maintenance and water
- Gym equipment servicing and replacement
- Elevator maintenance and contracts
- Security guard salaries and CCTV maintenance
- Building insurance (common areas only)
- Facade cleaning and building exterior

Reserve Fund (typically 10-15% of total budget):
- Major structural repairs
- Equipment replacement (chillers, pumps, generators)
- Roof and façade refurbishment
- Common area renovation

PAYING SERVICE CHARGES
- Paid quarterly or annually
- DEWA (utility) charges billed separately
- Late payment: Interest penalties may apply
- Unpaid charges: Owner may be blacklisted, unable to sell/transfer property

OWNERS ASSOCIATION (OA)
Every jointly owned development must have a registered Owners Association:
- Elected from property owners
- Registered with RERA
- Hires Management Company to execute decisions
- Annual general meeting (AGM) required
- Financial reports must be audited

OWNERS RIGHTS
- Vote on OA decisions
- Review financial statements
- Challenge unreasonable service charges at RERA
- Attend and speak at AGMs
- Receive 30 days notice before AGM
