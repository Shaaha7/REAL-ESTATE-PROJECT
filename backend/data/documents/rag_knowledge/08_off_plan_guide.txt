OFF-PLAN PROPERTY PURCHASING GUIDE — DUBAI
===========================================

WHAT IS OFF-PLAN?
Off-plan properties are sold before construction is complete, often during or before the launch phase.
Buyers purchase based on floor plans, renders, and show apartments.

ADVANTAGES OF OFF-PLAN
1. Lower entry price (typically 15-30% below completed market value)
2. Flexible payment plans (typically 10/20/30/40% over construction)
3. Higher ROI potential through capital appreciation during construction
4. New with warranties (5-year structural, 2-year MEP, 1-year finishing)
5. Choice of unit, floor, view, and finishes (if bought early)
6. No immediate mortgage required for payments during construction

RISKS OF OFF-PLAN
1. Construction delays (common in Dubai, 6-24 months average delay)
2. Developer financial difficulties
3. Quality not matching renders
4. Market changes during construction
5. Difficulty reselling during construction (requires NOC)
6. Mortgage not available until completion (in most cases)

HOW TO VERIFY DEVELOPER CREDIBILITY
1. RERA registration: Check RERA project number on Dubai REST app
2. Escrow account: Verify DLD-approved escrow (funds protected)
3. Developer track record: Research past projects, delivery timelines
4. Financial health: Check if publicly listed, awards, partnerships
5. Construction progress: Visit site, request updates

TYPICAL PAYMENT PLAN STRUCTURES
Construction Plan: 70:30
- 70% during construction (in installments)
- 30% on handover

Post-Handover Plan: 60:40
- 60% during construction
- 40% over 1-3 years after handover

Launch Plan: 10:90
- 10% on booking
- 90% on completion (high demand projects)

DOCUMENTS REQUIRED TO PURCHASE OFF-PLAN
- Valid passport (original)
- Completed application form
- Initial booking fee (typically 5-10% of unit price)
- Signed SPA (Sales and Purchase Agreement) within 2 weeks

OFF-PLAN SPA CONTENTS
The SPA must include:
- Unit number, type, size, floor
- Total purchase price
- Payment schedule and due dates
- Expected handover date
- Penalty clause for developer delays
- Specifications and finishes
- Post-handover warranty terms
- Conditions for cancellation

CANCELLATION POLICY (DEVELOPER DEFAULT)
If developer cancels or significantly delays:
- RERA may intervene and refund buyer
- Escrow funds must be returned
- Legal recourse through RERA dispute resolution

RESELLING OFF-PLAN PROPERTY
- Requires developer NOC (typically AED 5,000-15,000)
- Seller must pay any outstanding installments
- Buyer takes over payment plan
- DLD registration through interim registration
