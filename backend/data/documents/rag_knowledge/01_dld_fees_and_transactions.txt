DUBAI LAND DEPARTMENT (DLD) — FEES AND TRANSACTION GUIDE
==========================================================

PROPERTY TRANSFER FEE
The Dubai Land Department charges a transfer fee of 4% of the property purchase price.
This fee is typically split equally between buyer (2%) and seller (2%) by mutual agreement,
though the full 4% may be borne by one party based on contract terms.

Additional administrative fees apply:
- Properties valued up to AED 500,000: AED 4,000 admin fee
- Properties valued above AED 500,000: AED 580 admin fee (knowledge fee) + AED 4,000 registration

MORTGAGE REGISTRATION FEE
If the property is purchased with mortgage financing:
- 0.25% of the total loan amount
- Minimum fee: AED 1,000
- Maximum fee: No cap
Example: AED 1,500,000 mortgage = AED 3,750 mortgage registration fee

TITLE DEED ISSUANCE
- Title deed fee: AED 250
- Map issuance: AED 250 (for land) or AED 100 (for unit)
- Typing fee: AED 4,000 for non-off-plan properties

OFF-PLAN REGISTRATION (OQOOD)
For properties purchased directly from developers:
- Registration fee: 4% of sale price
- Oqood registration: AED 3,010 (villas/townhouses) or AED 1,020 (apartments)
- This registers the Sales and Purchase Agreement (SPA) on the DLD system

NOC (NO OBJECTION CERTIFICATE) FROM DEVELOPER
Required before title deed transfer in managed communities:
- Fee range: AED 500 to AED 5,000 (developer-dependent)
- Processing time: 5 to 15 working days
- Outstanding service charges must be cleared before NOC is issued
- Without NOC, DLD will not process the transfer

VALUATION FEE
For mortgage properties, a DLD-approved valuation is required:
- Independent valuation cost: AED 2,500 to AED 3,500
- Bank-arranged valuation may be included in mortgage processing fee

IMPORTANT TIMELINES
- Title deed registration must be completed within 30 days of signing the MOU
- Off-plan SPA must be registered within 30 days of signing
- Mortgage must be registered within 30 days of title deed transfer
