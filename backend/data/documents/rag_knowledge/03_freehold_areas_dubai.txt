DUBAI FREEHOLD PROPERTY OWNERSHIP GUIDE
=========================================

WHAT IS FREEHOLD OWNERSHIP?
Freehold ownership gives the buyer full ownership rights over the property and the land it sits on,
indefinitely. Foreign nationals (non-GCC) can purchase freehold properties in designated zones.

DESIGNATED FREEHOLD AREAS IN DUBAI
The Government of Dubai has designated specific zones where foreign nationals may own property:

PREMIUM FREEHOLD ZONES:
1. Dubai Marina — Waterfront apartments, high-rise living, marina views
2. Downtown Dubai — Adjacent to Burj Khalifa and Dubai Mall
3. Palm Jumeirah — Iconic artificial island, villas and apartments
4. Jumeirah Beach Residence (JBR) — Beachfront living
5. Emirates Hills — Ultra-luxury gated villa community
6. Dubai Hills Estate — Golf community, family-oriented

ESTABLISHED RESIDENTIAL FREEHOLD:
7. Jumeirah Lake Towers (JLT) — Mixed-use, metro access
8. Business Bay — Commercial and residential towers
9. DIFC — Dubai International Financial Centre
10. Jumeirah Village Circle (JVC) — Affordable apartments
11. Jumeirah Village Triangle (JVT)
12. Dubai Silicon Oasis — Tech park community
13. Al Barsha South (various clusters)
14. Mirdif (selected developments)

VILLA COMMUNITIES:
15. Arabian Ranches 1, 2, and 3
16. The Springs and The Meadows
17. The Lakes and The Greens
18. Mudon

20. Tilal Al Ghaf

EMERGING FREEHOLD AREAS:
21. Dubai South (Expo City Dubai area)
22. Dubailand

24. Discovery Gardens
25. Jumeirah Golf Estates

LEASEHOLD vs FREEHOLD
- Freehold: Full ownership, no time limit
- Leasehold (99-year): Right to use and occupy, land owned by government/developer
- Usufruct: Right to use for up to 99 years
- Musataha: Right to build on land for up to 50 years

OWNERSHIP RESTRICTIONS
- Foreign nationals cannot own in non-designated zones
- GCC nationals can own in all residential areas
- Companies can own if registered in UAE or specific free zones
- Jointly held properties: Each owner on the title deed
