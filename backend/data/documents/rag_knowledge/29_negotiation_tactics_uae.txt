ADVANCED NEGOTIATION TACTICS — UAE PROPERTY
============================================

KNOWING YOUR POSITION

Buyer has MORE power when:
- Property listed for 60+ days (seller is motivated)
- Market has lots of similar properties
- Interest rates are high (fewer mortgage buyers)
- You are a cash buyer (no mortgage risk)
- Seller has personal urgency (relocation, divorce, financial need)

Seller has MORE power when:
- New, just listed property
- Unique property (no comparable)
- Strong market (multiple interested buyers)
- Off-plan launch (developer rarely discounts)

HOW TO CHECK IF SELLER IS MOTIVATED
1. Check listing date on Property Finder/Bayut
2. Check how many times price was reduced (portal history)
3. Ask agent: "Has the seller had any offers? Why didn't they proceed?"
4. Check DLD transactions: If seller bought recently at lower price, less room to negotiate
5. Ask: "Is the seller flexible on timing?" (flexibility = motivation)

CASH BUYER ADVANTAGE
Cash buyer = no mortgage = faster, less risk for seller
Typical cash discount: 5-10% below asking price
Use it: "I am a cash buyer. I can transfer within 3 weeks. I'd like to offer [X]."
Prepare: Have bank statements or letter from bank showing funds

ANCHOR LOW — FIRST OFFER STRATEGY
Never offer asking price as first offer (unless clearly underpriced).
Start 10-15% below asking price.
Seller will counter. You meet in the middle.
Example: Asking AED 2,000,000 → Offer AED 1,750,000 → Counter AED 1,950,000 → Settle AED 1,850,000

WHAT TO INCLUDE IN NEGOTIATION (BEYOND PRICE)
Price isn't everything. Also negotiate:
- Payment timing (split into fewer cheques = better for you)
- Furniture/appliances included (ask for kitchen appliances, AC units)
- Handover date (if you need specific timing)
- NOC fee (who pays — usually seller, but can be shared)
- Agent commission (ask seller agent if they'll accept 1.5% — some will)
- Outstanding service charges (ensure seller clears all before transfer)

DLD TRANSACTION DATA — YOUR SECRET WEAPON
Before making any offer, check ACTUAL recent sales:
Website: Property Monitor, DXB Interact, or Dubaipropertydata.com
Search: Same building or same community
Filter: Last 3-6 months
This shows REAL prices paid, not asking prices.
If similar unit sold for AED 1,700,000 three months ago, you know AED 2,000,000 asking is too high.

OFF-PLAN NEGOTIATION (DEVELOPER)
Developers rarely discount headline price. But you can negotiate:
- Floor level (higher = more view = higher price, ask for mid-floor at high-floor view)
- Payment plan (ask for longer post-handover plan)
- Waiver of DLD fee (some developers absorb the 4% DLD during promotions)
- Waiver of admin fees
- Parking spaces (sometimes extra parking is negotiable)
- Storage unit included
- Early handover penalty clause (important — get in writing)
- Post-handover payment plan (pay 30-40% after you get keys)

MULTIPLE OFFER SITUATION
If agent says "we have another offer":
- Could be true or a tactic to create urgency
- Ask: "Is the other offer above or below asking price?"
- If you want the property: Make your best offer immediately, with short deadline
- Include in offer: "This offer is valid for 48 hours only"
- Don't get emotional — walk away if above budget

WALKING AWAY
The most powerful negotiation tactic.
"I really like this property but at AED 1,900,000 it doesn't work for me. My maximum is AED 1,780,000. If the seller can accept that, I'm ready to proceed immediately. Otherwise, I'll continue looking."
Then actually walk away. Wait 24-48 hours. Seller often calls back.

WHEN NOT TO NEGOTIATE HARD
- Ultra-luxury (AED 10M+): Different rules, relationship matters more than aggressive negotiation
- Motivated buyer market (when you need the property urgently): Negotiate too hard and lose it
- Unique one-of-a-kind properties: Limited supply means less leverage

USING A BUYER'S AGENT
In Dubai, having your own agent (buyer's agent) is advantageous:
- They know which sellers are motivated
- They have relationships with selling agents
- They can negotiate on your behalf professionally
- Cost: Typically 2% (you pay), but they should save you more than that
