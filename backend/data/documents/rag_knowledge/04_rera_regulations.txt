RERA — REAL ESTATE REGULATORY AGENCY COMPLETE GUIDE
====================================================

WHAT IS RERA?
RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Agency) is the regulatory arm of the Dubai Land Department (DLD).
Established in 2007, RERA governs all real estate activity in Dubai including:
- Developer registration and project approvals
- Broker and agent licensing
- Rental regulations (RERA rental index)
- Dispute resolution
- Off-plan escrow management

DEVELOPER REQUIREMENTS
Before selling any off-plan property, developers must:
1. Register the project with RERA (get RERA project number)
2. Obtain all construction permits from relevant authorities
3. Open a RERA-approved escrow account (funds held separately)
4. Show proof of land ownership or long-term lease
5. Obtain DLD approval for sales launch
6. Register all sales staff as licensed real estate brokers

ESCROW ACCOUNT REQUIREMENTS
- Developer cannot access escrow funds until construction milestones are reached
- DLD appoints independent escrow auditors
- Buyer funds are protected if developer defaults
- Escrow releases: 20% on foundation, 30% on structure, 30% on completion, 20% on handover

BROKER AND AGENT LICENSING
All real estate brokers in Dubai must:
- Pass the RERA exam (Certified Real Estate Broker)
- Register with Dubai Land Department
- Obtain a RERA broker card (renewed annually)
- Work under a licensed real estate company (brokerage)
- Display RERA card number on all marketing materials

HOW TO VERIFY BROKER CREDENTIALS
- Dubai REST app: Search broker by name or RERA number
- DLD website: Broker verification portal
- Report unlicensed brokers: 800-DLD (800-353)

RENTAL REGULATIONS
Rental Increase Calculator: RERA provides an official rental index
- If current rent is within 10% of RERA index: 0% increase allowed
- If rent is 11-20% below index: Maximum 5% increase
- If rent is 21-30% below index: Maximum 10% increase
- If rent is 31-40% below index: Maximum 15% increase
- If rent is more than 40% below index: Maximum 20% increase

EJARI REGISTRATION
All tenancy contracts must be registered on Ejari (RERA online system):
- Mandatory for all rental agreements in Dubai
- Required for: DEWA connection, residency visa renewal, school enrollment
- Registration fee: AED 195 (service centers) or AED 155 (online)
- Both landlord and tenant must register within 30 days of signing

STRATA LAW (OWNERS ASSOCIATIONS)
- All jointly owned property communities must have an Owners Association (OA)
- OA manages common areas, collects service charges
- Annual budgets must be approved by RERA
- Service charges based on RERA-approved rates per square foot

DISPUTE RESOLUTION
The Rental Dispute Settlement Centre (RDSC):
- Filing fee: 3.5% of annual rent (min AED 250, max AED 20,000)
- Handles: Eviction disputes, rent increase disagreements, maintenance disputes
- Process: Filing → Mediation (30 days) → Judgment (30 days)
