UAE BANKING FOR EXPATS — COMPLETE GUIDE
========================================

OPENING A UAE BANK ACCOUNT (EXPAT)

Requirements:
- Valid passport
- UAE residence visa
- Emirates ID
- Proof of address (utility bill or tenancy contract)
- Salary certificate or employment letter
- 3-6 months bank statements from home country (some banks)

Best banks for expats:
1. Emirates NBD — largest UAE bank, best app, widespread ATMs
2. ADCB (Abu Dhabi Commercial Bank) — good mortgage rates, TouchPoints rewards
3. FAB (First Abu Dhabi Bank) — premium banking, good wealth management
4. Mashreq — good for self-employed, faster account opening
5. HSBC UAE — ideal for international clients, global transfers easy
6. Standard Chartered UAE — good for South Asian clients
7. RAKBANK — good for small business owners

Account types:
- Current account (no interest, cheque book)
- Savings account (interest 0.5-2% pa)
- Fixed deposit (2.5-4.5% pa for 1-3 years)
- Islamic accounts (profit-sharing, no interest — for Sharia compliance)

MORTGAGE BANKS IN UAE — BEST RATES 2024

Top mortgage lenders (ranked by rate competitiveness):
1. Emirates NBD: From 3.99% fixed (1 year), variable EIBOR+1.5%
2. FAB: From 3.89% fixed, competitive for AED 3M+
3. ADCB: From 4.15% fixed, good for salaried employees
4. Mashreq: From 4.25% fixed, flexible for self-employed
5. HSBC: From 4.35% fixed, good for international clients
6. DIB (Dubai Islamic Bank): From 4.1% (Islamic Ijara product)
7. ADIB (Abu Dhabi Islamic Bank): Competitive Islamic mortgage

EIBOR (Emirates Interbank Offered Rate):
UAE mortgages are often EIBOR-linked (variable rate).
EIBOR 3-month rate (2024): approximately 4.8-5.2%
Variable mortgage = EIBOR + bank spread (1.5-2.5%)
Variable rate effective: 6.3-7.7% (2024 environment)
Fixed rates (1-5 years): 3.99-5.5% then revert to variable

MORTGAGE ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
Salaried employees:
- Minimum salary: AED 10,000/month (most banks)
- Some banks require: AED 15,000 (for properties above AED 1.5M)
- Employment: Minimum 3-6 months with current employer
- DBR (Debt Burden Ratio): Must not exceed 50% of monthly income

Self-employed:
- Minimum 2 years business operation
- Audited financials for 2 years
- Higher scrutiny, may need higher down payment
- Minimum income: AED 25,000/month from business

Non-residents (mortgages):
- Very limited options
- Usually 50% LTV maximum
- Higher rates: 5-7%
- Mashreq, ADCB, RAKBank offer non-resident mortgages

USEFUL CALCULATIONS

Monthly mortgage payment formula:
Monthly = [P × r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1]
Where: P = loan amount, r = monthly rate, n = months

Example: AED 1,500,000 mortgage at 4.5% fixed, 25 years:
Monthly rate = 4.5% / 12 = 0.375%
Monthly payment = AED 8,322/month

Example: AED 3,000,000 mortgage at 4.5%, 25 years:
Monthly payment = AED 16,644/month

Example: AED 5,000,000 mortgage at 4.5%, 25 years:
Monthly payment = AED 27,740/month

MORTGAGE PROCESS TIMELINE
Day 1-3: Submit pre-approval application with documents
Day 3-7: Bank issues pre-approval (in-principle approval)
Day 7-14: Find property, sign MOU
Day 14-21: Bank orders independent valuation (AED 2,500-3,500)
Day 21-30: Final offer letter issued
Day 30-45: Mortgage registration at DLD (0.25% of loan amount)
Day 45-60: Transfer and title deed in buyer's name
