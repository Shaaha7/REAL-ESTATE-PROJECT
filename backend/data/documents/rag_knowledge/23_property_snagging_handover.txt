PROPERTY SNAGGING AND HANDOVER — COMPLETE GUIDE
================================================

WHAT IS SNAGGING?
Snagging is the process of inspecting a new property before accepting handover from the developer.
You identify defects, issues, and unfinished items — developer must fix them before final handover.

WHY SNAGGING IS CRITICAL:
- New properties in UAE often have 50-200+ snag items
- Once you accept handover and sign, developer liability reduces significantly
- Warranty periods: 1 year finishing, 2 years MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing), 10 years structure
- The best time to negotiate fixes is BEFORE you accept keys

SNAGGING PROCESS

Step 1: Receive handover notice from developer
Developer sends NOC and invitation to collect keys.
Do NOT rush to collect keys — take 2-4 weeks to do proper snagging.

Step 2: Hire a professional snagging company (recommended)
Cost: AED 1,500 – 3,500 for apartment | AED 3,000 – 8,000 for villa
They use thermal cameras, moisture meters, level checkers
Report: 50-300 page detailed PDF with photos of every defect

Top snagging companies in UAE:
- Snag My Home
- Property Snagger
- Cavendish Maxwell Snagging
- RICS-certified surveyors

Step 3: Submit snag list to developer
Developer has 30-60 days typically to rectify defects
Keep all communication in writing (email)

Step 4: Re-inspection
After developer claims fixes are done, re-inspect every item
Only sign final acceptance when satisfied

COMMON SNAG ITEMS TO CHECK

STRUCTURAL:
□ Cracks in walls, floors, ceilings
□ Doors and windows not closing/locking properly
□ Uneven floors (use spirit level)
□ Gaps around window frames and door frames

FINISHES:
□ Tiles: chips, cracks, poor grouting, hollow tiles (tap each one)
□ Paint: streaks, missed patches, wrong colour, drips
□ Woodwork: scratches, poor fitting, gaps in skirting boards
□ Kitchen cabinets: alignment, hinges, soft-close not working

MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing):
□ All light switches work
□ All power sockets work (test with phone charger)
□ AC: all units blow cold, thermostats work, no water leaks
□ Plumbing: all taps work, no leaks under sinks
□ Toilets: flush properly, no leaks at base
□ Water pressure: adequate in all taps and showers
□ Hot water: reaches all taps within reasonable time

SAFETY:
□ Smoke detectors installed and working
□ Fire suppression system (if applicable)
□ Balcony railings: secure, correct height
□ Emergency lighting works

POST-HANDOVER CHECKLIST:
□ DEWA connection (electricity and water): Apply at dewa.gov.ae (AED 1,100 deposit for apartment)
□ Building access cards/fobs
□ Parking space allocated correctly
□ Mailbox key
□ Owners Association registration
□ Insurance: Take out buildings insurance (contents insurance for furniture)
□ Change all locks (especially for resale properties)

WARRANTY CLAIMS PROCESS:
Year 1-2: Contact developer's after-sales team directly
Year 2-10: Structural defects — developer legally responsible under UAE law
After warranty: Your responsibility or Owners Association (common areas)

RESALE PROPERTY INSPECTION CHECKLIST:
For used/resale properties, also check:
□ History of water damage or leaks (look at ceiling, under sinks)
□ AC units age and condition
□ Electrical panel: fuses, circuit breakers
□ Building age and major maintenance history
□ Outstanding service charges (must be zero at transfer)
□ Any legal disputes or liens on the property (check with DLD)
