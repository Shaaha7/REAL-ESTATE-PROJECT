DUBAI PROPERTY PRICE HISTORY AND TRENDS 2010-2025
==================================================

HISTORICAL PRICE TIMELINE

2008-2009: GLOBAL FINANCIAL CRISIS
- Dubai property crashed 50-60% from peak
- Nakheel (Palm developer) defaulted on AED 59B debt
- Many off-plan projects cancelled
- Lesson: Dubai market is not immune to global shocks

2012-2014: RECOVERY AND BOOM
- Prices recovered to 2008 levels by 2014
- Expo 2020 bid win (November 2013) drove confidence
- Foreign investment surged
- Villas in prime areas: +30-40% in 18 months

2014-2020: CORRECTION AND STAGNATION
- Oversupply: Too many new units delivered
- Oil price crash (2014-2016) reduced GCC buyer confidence
- Prices fell 20-30% from 2014 peak
- Covid-19 (2020): Brief further dip, then rapid recovery

2020-2021: COVID CATALYST (UNEXPECTED BOOM)
- Dubai handled COVID well — open economy, vaccination drive
- Global ultra-wealthy relocated: Russian, Indian, European, American buyers
- Villa prices: +15-20% in 2021
- Apartment prices: +8-12% in 2021

2022: SUSTAINED BOOM
- Record transaction volumes: 86,000+ transactions (new record)
- Total value: AED 300B+
- Palm Jumeirah villas: +40-50% year-on-year
- Emirates Hills mansions: +60-80% from 2020 lows

2023: RECORD BREAKING
- Transactions: 120,000+ (new all-time record)
- Value: AED 400B+
- Off-plan sales: 60% of total market
- Foreign buyer share: 65%
- Top nationalities: Indian, British, Russian, Chinese, Pakistani

2024: CONTINUED GROWTH (MODERATED)
- Expected: +8-12% price growth (slower than 2022-2023 surge)
- Villa prices stabilising at new highs
- Apartment market: New supply creating some pressure
- Ultra-luxury (AED 10M+): Still very strong demand

KEY PRICE BENCHMARKS (AED PER SQFT)

Dubai Marina Apartments:
- 2012: AED 900-1,100/sqft
- 2020: AED 850-1,000/sqft (fell from 2014 peak)
- 2024: AED 1,500-2,200/sqft (+65-100% from 2020)

Palm Jumeirah Villas:
- 2012: AED 1,500-2,000/sqft
- 2020: AED 1,800-2,500/sqft
- 2024: AED 4,000-8,000/sqft (+100-200% from 2020)

Emirates Hills Villas:
- 2020: AED 2,000-3,500/sqft
- 2024: AED 5,000-9,000/sqft (+150% from 2020)

JVC Apartments:
- 2020: AED 500-650/sqft
- 2024: AED 750-1,100/sqft (+50-70% from 2020)

DRIVERS OF CURRENT MARKET STRENGTH
1. Net migration: Dubai population grew 200,000+ in 2022-2023
2. Tax advantage: 0% income tax vs 40-50% in UK/Europe
3. Safe haven: Russia-Ukraine war drove Russian capital to Dubai
4. Indian tech wealth: India's startup boom created new HNWIs buying Dubai
5. Crypto wealth: Many crypto millionaires chose Dubai (crypto-friendly)
6. Golden Visa: Permanently relocating vs just investing
7. Quality of life: Infrastructure, safety, weather, lifestyle

2025-2026 OUTLOOK
Positive indicators:
- Expo City becoming business hub (5,000+ companies)
- Al Maktoum Airport expansion (will be world's largest)
- Population target: 6 million by 2040 (from 3.5M now)
- New tourism infrastructure: 20M+ visitors targeted

Risks:
- Global recession impact
- USD strengthening (AED pegged — makes Dubai more expensive for non-USD buyers)
- New supply: 40,000+ units expected in 2024-2025
- Geopolitical risk in region
